How our Adaptivity works

We believe that teachers should have complete control over their students’ learning experience. That’s why at Smart Sparrow – Adaptive eLearning is not about some sort of magic, super complex, black box algorithm. Instead you control how it can adapt to individual students, by addressing individual misconceptions. We call that control Trap states, for when you want to trap the student’s behaviour and respond in real time. Trap States are a powerful concept that will change the way you teach.

Simple Building Blocks

The building blocks of Trap States are conditions and actions. When the conditions are fulfilled, the actions are executed. Conditions can target any Adaptivity Factor, for example, whether the student made a mistake, took a long time, or has not mastered a concept. We call these Adaptivity Factors. Actions can include: giving feedback or changing the learning pathway.

Adaptive Feedback

The best engagement is the right information at the right time. Students make mistakes: they forget what was said in the lecture, they confuse terms, they use the wrong formula. When this happens teachers provide feedback to students as they learn in order address a misconception. Adaptive eLearning systems allow teachers to do the same by providing instant intelligent feedback exactly when it is needed.

Adaptive Pathways

Each student is different. They learn at varying rates, have different levels of knowledge, and different misconceptions. Teachers need to cater to the individual student. For example, if a student already knows what they are doing they should skip ahead to a more challenging task, or if a student is struggling and missing some required knowledge then they should be directed to resources that help them understand that particular concept.

Adapt your Teaching

A good teacher continually improves and adapts their teaching. Every time a lecture is given, it’s an improved version based on past experience teaching that subject. Teachers regularly adapt homework and assignments based on their students’ performance. It’s all about continually learning how to teach better.
